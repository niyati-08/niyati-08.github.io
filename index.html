Welcome

“RNA structure shapes biological function.”

Welcome to the JAIN Lab. Our research focuses on RNA structural biology, solution-state NMR spectroscopy, RNA–protein interactions, RNA–ligand recognition, and biologically important non-coding RNA structures.

We combine NMR spectroscopy, biophysical assays, RNA molecular biology, and collaborative computational approaches to understand how structured RNA elements contribute to regulation in viral and cellular systems.

Research Themes

RNA Structural Biology

Structural and dynamic studies of functional RNA motifs using NMR and biophysical methods.

RNA–Protein Interactions

Mechanistic studies of RNA recognition by RNA-binding proteins in cellular and viral systems.

Non-coding RNAs

Structure-function studies of long non-coding RNAs, including MALAT1-associated RNA motifs.

RNA–Ligand Recognition

Understanding how small molecules interact with structured RNAs and influence stability or function.
